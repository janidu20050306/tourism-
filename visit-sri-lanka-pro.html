Greetings

Say "Ayubowan" (meaning "May you live long") with hands pressed together. This respectful greeting is welcomed throughout Sri Lanka.

Remove Shoes

Always remove shoes before entering temples, homes, and many guesthouses. Watch for the "no shoes" sign and follow local behaviour.

Dress Modestly

Cover shoulders and knees when visiting religious sites. Carry a sarong or light shawl for unexpected temple visits. This is strictly enforced.

Photography

Never pose with your back to a Buddha statue â€” this is considered disrespectful. Always ask permission before photographing people.

Food Etiquette

Eating with your right hand is customary. Many Sri Lankans eat on banana leaves. Be adventurous â€” the local cuisine is extraordinary.

Gentle bargaining is accepted in markets and with tuk-tuk drivers. Always be respectful and friendly â€” Sri Lankans are warm and generous hosts.
